HIPAA Privacy and Security Rule - Selected Excerpts (Summary)

Purpose:
This file provides a concise, plain-language summary of selected HIPAA sections used for retrieval grounding in compliance demos.

Section focus:
- 45 CFR 164.502: Uses and disclosures of protected health information
- 45 CFR 164.514: De-identification and re-identification
- 45 CFR 164.312: Technical safeguards

164.502 (Uses and disclosures):
- Covered entities and business associates may use or disclose PHI only as permitted or required by the rule.
- Uses beyond treatment, payment, and health care operations generally require authorization unless an exception applies.
- Minimum necessary principles apply in many routine disclosure contexts.

164.514 (De-identification):
- Information is not individually identifiable when it has been de-identified according to recognized methods.
- Two common methods are used in practice:
  1) Expert determination
  2) Safe Harbor removal of specified identifiers
- If de-identification is reversed or linkable keys are mishandled, compliance risk increases.

Safe Harbor identifier set (18 categories):
1. Names
2. Geographic subdivisions smaller than a state (with limited exceptions)
3. All elements of dates (except year) directly related to an individual
4. Telephone numbers
5. Fax numbers
6. Email addresses
7. Social Security numbers
8. Medical record numbers
9. Health plan beneficiary numbers
10. Account numbers
11. Certificate or license numbers
12. Vehicle identifiers and serial numbers, including license plates
13. Device identifiers and serial numbers
14. Web URLs
15. Internet protocol (IP) addresses
16. Biometric identifiers, including fingerprints and voiceprints
17. Full-face photos and comparable images
18. Any other unique identifying number, characteristic, or code

164.312 (Technical safeguards):
- Access control: implement unique user identification and controlled access paths.
- Audit controls: implement hardware/software mechanisms to record and examine system activity.
- Integrity controls: protect PHI from improper alteration or destruction.
- Person/entity authentication: verify users and systems accessing PHI.
- Transmission security: guard against unauthorized PHI access during network transmission.

Compliance implication for code review:
- Systems handling PHI should enforce authentication, authorization, encryption in transit, and robust auditing.
- Logging and telemetry pipelines must avoid exposing direct identifiers.
