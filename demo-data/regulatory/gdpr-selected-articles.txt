GDPR - Selected Articles and Recital (Summary)

Purpose:
This summary highlights selected GDPR requirements commonly referenced in PII/PHI handling reviews.

Selected provisions:
- Article 5: Principles relating to processing of personal data
- Article 6: Lawfulness of processing
- Article 9: Special categories of personal data
- Article 15: Right of access
- Article 17: Right to erasure
- Article 25: Data protection by design and by default
- Article 32: Security of processing
- Article 35: Data protection impact assessment (DPIA)
- Recital 71: Automated decision-making and profiling considerations

Article 5 (Principles):
- Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
- Collection should be purpose-limited and data-minimized.
- Data should be accurate, retained no longer than necessary, and protected with integrity/confidentiality controls.

Article 6 (Lawfulness):
- Processing requires a valid lawful basis (for example, consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests where applicable).

Article 9 (Special categories):
- Processing special categories (including many health, genetic, and biometric contexts) is prohibited unless specific conditions and safeguards are met.

Article 15 (Access right):
- Data subjects can request confirmation, access, and information about processing activities.

Article 17 (Erasure right):
- Data subjects can request deletion in qualifying circumstances, subject to legal exceptions.

Article 25 (By design/default):
- Controllers must implement privacy safeguards in system design and default settings.

Article 32 (Security):
- Appropriate technical and organizational measures are required, including controls such as encryption, confidentiality, resilience, and restoration processes.

Article 35 (DPIA) - key gap indicator:
- Where processing is likely to result in high risk to rights and freedoms, controllers must perform a Data Protection Impact Assessment before processing.
- High-risk profiling, large-scale sensitive processing, and novel technology use can trigger DPIA obligations.

Recital 71 (Profiling context):
- Safeguards are expected in automated decision-making scenarios, including meaningful protections for data subjects.

Compliance implication for code review:
- Systems with high-risk profiling should demonstrate DPIA evidence.
- APIs should support data subject access and deletion workflows.
- Sensitive data processing must show lawful basis and strong security controls.
